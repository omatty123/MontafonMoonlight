# Editorial Notes

This file records substantive interventions made while assembling the edited
Korean corpus and aligning the English manuscript. Diplomatic source
transcriptions remain unchanged in `source-transcriptions/`; corrected reading
texts appear in `chapters/`.

## Chapter 21 — 아바도와 메시앙과의 만남

The MediaBuddha page contains a passage after “고맙습니다” describing the severe
deadline for Willi’s commission, his retreat to Hittisau in the Bregenzerwald,
and two weeks of work in a studio in Lützelflüh in Switzerland’s Emmental. This
material was absent from the existing English chapter.

The omitted passage was restored to `content/chapter-21.html` within the same
paragraph as “As a result of these efforts,” preserving the Korean paragraph
structure. Standard geographic spellings were confirmed against the official
municipal sites:

- Hittisau: https://www.hittisau.at/kontakt/
- Lützelflüh: https://www.luetzelflueh.ch/de/

## Chapter 22 — 로마 여행 시작

Two corrections are applied to the edited corpus and English manuscript. The
saved page transcription preserves the MediaBuddha wording exactly.

### `바시리아인들아` → `아시리아인들아`

The printed `바시리아인들아` is treated as a typographical error. Martial’s
poem contrasts Egypt’s pyramids with Assyria and Babylon, so the intended Korean
is `아시리아인들아` (“O Assyrians”). The English manuscript already used “O
Assyrians” and required no change.

Reference: https://www.poetryintranslation.com/PITBR/Latin/Martial.php

### `제1차 세계대전` → `제2차 세계대전`

The MediaBuddha page says Mussolini declared Italy’s entry into World War I from
Piazza Venezia. The relevant declaration occurred on June 10, 1940, when
Mussolini announced Italy’s entry into World War II against Britain and France.
The edited Korean corpus and English Chapter 22 therefore use World War II.

References:

- https://www.archivioluce.com/10-giugno-1940-litalia-e-in-guerra/
- https://www.raicultura.it/storia/accadde-oggi/LItalia-in-guerra-63391ff9-60e2-404a-95f8-75f6d60a60cf.html
