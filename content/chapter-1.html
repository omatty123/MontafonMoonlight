Vienna Bound Plane

The cabin lights dim to a soft polar blue. Outside, the wing cuts an ellipse through cloud. You rehearse a sentence in German you will never say, then fold it into your pocket with the boarding stub. Across the aisle, a child sketches mountains that look like teeth; the mother nods to the rhythm of the engines. Somewhere over the Alps, the pilot banks and the moon rolls like a coin.

The stranger beside you asks where home is. You point at the map on the seatback screen—an arrow moving toward Vienna— then shrug. Home, today, is a pressurized room where you can finally decide.
