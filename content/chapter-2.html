Three Phonecalls

The first call arrives before breakfast, from a number that still lives in your muscle memory. The kettle clicks off; you let it cool while the voice on the line warms old rooms. The second call rides the afternoon—airport PA systems, a crowd like weather. By the third, night has drawn the lines on the map darker than they should be.

Three versions of the same request: come back, keep going, or turn somewhere new. You stack the choices like postcards and realize they all show the same horizon. You don’t need to choose a city. You need to choose a sentence. You begin with “I’m listening.”
