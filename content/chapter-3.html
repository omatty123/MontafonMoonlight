Resolution

Dawn at the hill circle. Dew slicks the grass; stones keep the oldest calendar. You place your phone on a flat rock and let it sleep—no calls today. The apology arrives as a breath, then a paragraph. It lands, not like a verdict, but like weather clearing.

When the sun finds the notch between two standing stones, you say the sentence you carried across an ocean. It is not dramatic. It is finally true. The plane ticket, once heavy, is just paper again. You take the long way down, already composing a message that starts with “Arrived.”
