Jeong Chanju 정찬주

Korean novelist and essayist whose work returns again and again to Buddhist practice, historical memory, and the lives of people who keep faith with their inner calling.

Biography

Born in 1953 in Boseong, Jeollanam-do, Jeong Chanju graduated from Dongguk University with a degree in Korean Language and Literature. After a brief period teaching Korean, he began an editorial career at the monthly Buddhist Thought.

For more than a decade he worked at Samtoh Publishing, where he helped produce the works of Venerable Beopjeong and became the monk’s close lay disciple. From Beopjeong he received the Dharma name Muyeom (無染), “unstained,” along with the teaching to live in the world without being tainted by it.

Since receiving the Korean Literature New Writer’s Award in 1983, Jeong has cultivated a literary world attentive to historical figures, Buddhist practitioners, and the quiet places where a life ripens into its own form. He lives and writes at Ibuljae, his hermitage on Gyedang Mountain in Hwasun, Jeollanam-do.

Themes and Honors

Across more than one hundred titles, Jeong’s work explores Korea’s spiritual culture through journeys, hermitages, historical imagination, and portraits of exemplary lives.

His honors include the Haengwon Literary Award, Dongguk Literature Prize, Hwajeong Cultural Award, Ryu Juhyeon Literary Award, and Yusim Literary Award.
